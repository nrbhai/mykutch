Vijay Vilas Palace — Royal Summer Retreat

Vijay Vilas Palace is a magnificent summer palace of the Maharao of Kutch, located on the pristine beaches of Mandvi. Built between 1920 and 1929, this architectural marvel combines Rajput and Indo-Saracenic styles, featuring stunning red sandstone construction, intricate jalis (carved stone screens), beautiful domes, and a private beach. The palace has served as a filming location for famous Bollywood movies including "Lagaan" and "Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam."

📖 Learn More: For detailed information about Vijay Vilas Palace's history and architecture, visit the Wikipedia page .

History

The palace was commissioned during the reign of Maharao Khengarji III as a summer residence for his son and heir, Yuvraj Shri Vijayaraji, after whom it was named. Construction began in 1920 and was completed in 1929. After the devastating 2001 Gujarat earthquake damaged the Ranjit Vilas Palace in Bhuj, the royal family lived permanently at Vijay Vilas Palace. Today, the royal family uses it as a summer home while the ground floor houses a museum open to visitors.

Architectural Highlights

  • Red Sandstone Construction : The entire palace is built with beautiful red sandstone, referencing the palaces of Orchha and Datia.
  • Central Dome & Bengali Domes : Features a grand central high dome on pillars with smaller Bengali-style domes on the sides.
  • Intricate Jalis & Jharokas : Exquisite carved stone screens and windows created by master craftsmen from Jaipur, Bengal, Saurashtra, and local Kutchi artisans.
  • Colored Glass Work : Stunning stained glass windows and door panels that create beautiful light effects.
  • Rooftop Terrace : Open upper terrace offering panoramic views of the palace grounds and the Arabian Sea.
  • Landscaped Gardens : Set among beautiful gardens with water channels, marble fountains, and manicured lawns.

Visitor Information

  • Timings : Open daily. Check locally for specific hours and entry fees.
  • Duration : 1–2 hours recommended to explore the palace, museum, and grounds.
  • Museum : The ground floor features a museum with royal artifacts and historical displays.
  • Private Beach : The palace has its own private beach accessible to visitors.
  • Accommodation : The palace grounds include a separate heritage resort offering overnight stays.
  • Best Time : October to March for pleasant weather. Early morning or late afternoon offers the best light for photography.

Bollywood Connection

Vijay Vilas Palace has been a favorite filming location for Bollywood. Notable movies shot here include:

  • Lagaan (2001) : Aamir Khan's Oscar-nominated cricket epic featured the palace prominently.
  • Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam (1999) : Romantic scenes were filmed in the palace's stunning interiors.
  • Commando : Action sequences utilized the palace's dramatic architecture.
  • Several Gujarati films have also been shot at this magnificent location.
